Guidelines & Methodology

How every number on the Sales Rep Performance dashboard is calculated. Use this page as the single source of truth when reviewing a rep's scorecard, the leaderboard, or the team-level KPIs.

1Overview

The tracker captures, for every rep, four pieces of weekly forecast data plus actual closed revenue:

Commit Best Case Total Pipeline Closed Won

Each of these flows into one or more performance metrics. The dashboard then rolls them up per rep, per team, and aggregated across whatever Period you select (current month, last 3, last 6, YTD).

v18.1 — Multi-Deal Selection per Slot.
  • Pick more than one deal: Each rep can now check multiple deals under their Commit and under their Best Case. The picker is a checkbox list — Commit AED and Best Case AED sum automatically from the selection.
  • Rep-scoped deal list: Each row's dropdown only shows that rep's own HubSpot deals (fetched by their owner ID). Reps never see another rep's pipeline.
  • Running total + cross-slot guard: Sticky header shows "N selected · AED total" live as you tick boxes. A deal already picked in Commit is disabled in Best Case (and vice-versa) on the same row.
v18.0 — HubSpot-Driven Deal Picker.
  • Strict picker: Commit and Best Case deal names + amounts are selected from a read-only HubSpot dropdown — no more manual entry. Reps retain control over buyer signals, next actions, confidence, and other qualitative fields.
  • Hard block when disconnected: If HubSpot is not connected, the deal cells show a "Connect HubSpot" prompt instead of an entry field.
  • Lazy refresh: Deal lists fetch on first dropdown open per rep per session. Sync Now and Sync All Reps invalidate the cache so the next open pulls fresh data.
v16.0 — Direct Engagement Independence & Light Theme.
  • Standalone Direct Engagement: Now rendered as its own independent section in the forecast grid and commitment dashboard, no longer nested under Abu Dhabi.
  • Manual Outlook Sync: Added a dedicated button in the Demo Tracker toolbar for immediate sync.
  • Light Grey Theme: Modernized the interface with a light grey palette and Inter font stack for improved legibility.
Rule of thumb: all forecast metrics use the latest week with activity as a month-end snapshot. Closed Won uses the maximum value reported across weeks (because closed-won is cumulative — it only ever goes up within a month).

3Stage Weights

Every deal's forecast contribution is multiplied by a stage-based probability factor. These weights are the heart of the Weighted Forecast calculation.

Qualified
0.25
25% probability
Proposal
0.50
50% probability
Negotiation
0.75
75% probability
Weighted Forecast (per row)
weightedForecast = (Commit + BestCase) × StageWeight × StalePenalty
📌 Example — Fatima
  • Commit AED 200,000, Best Case AED 100,000, Stage = Proposal, deal is fresh (not stale).
  • weightedForecast = (200,000 + 100,000) × 0.50 × 1.0 = AED 150,000
  • If the same row were in Negotiation: 300,000 × 0.75 = AED 225,000
  • If still in Qualified: 300,000 × 0.25 = AED 75,000

4Aging & Stale-Deal Penalty

A deal is "stale" when it has stayed in the same stage for the same rep across consecutive weekly submissions for too long. The tracker walks backwards through your weekly history and counts how many consecutive weeks the same dealName + stage + rep combination has persisted.

Stale thresholds (per stage)
Qualified > 14 days · Proposal > 21 days · Negotiation > 30 days

When a row crosses its threshold:

  1. The row highlights red in the tracker grid.
  2. That row's Weighted Forecast is halved (StalePenalty = 0.5) — penalising rep optimism on deals that aren't moving.
📌 Example — Connor
  • "Aldar Properties" Commit appears in Negotiation for 5 consecutive weeks → ageEstimate = 5 × 7 = 35 days > 30 → stale.
  • Commit AED 500,000 in Negotiation: 500,000 × 0.75 × 0.5 = AED 187,500 instead of AED 375,000.

5Qualification Score (0–100)

Shown in the tracker grid next to each deal row. Combines the buyer-signal checkboxes (60% of the score) with stage progression (40% of the score).

Qualification Score
signalsPart = (checkedSignals ÷ 7) × 60
stagePart = (StageWeight ÷ 0.75) × 40
qualScore = round( signalsPart + stagePart )

There are 7 buyer signals in total: Budget Confirmed, Timeline Defined, Internal Champion, Demo Completed, Proposal Submitted, Decision-Maker Engaged, Competition Eliminated.

Stage maps to: Qualified → 13.3, Proposal → 26.7, Negotiation → 40.

6Sales Rep Performance Metrics

Every rep scorecard on the Performance tab shows four metrics. Each one answers a different question.

6.1 · Forecast Accuracy
Higher is better

How close the rep's Weighted Forecast ended up being to their actual Closed Won. Uses a symmetric formula so both over-forecasting and under-forecasting are penalised equally.

denom = max(weightedForecast, closedWon)
accuracy = 1 − |weightedForecast − closedWon| ÷ denom
≥ 85% Healthy 70 – 84% Watch < 70% Off-target
📌 Example — Marios
  • Weighted forecast (end of May): AED 420,000 · Closed Won: AED 380,000
  • denom = max(420k, 380k) = 420,000
  • accuracy = 1 − |420k − 380k| ÷ 420k = 1 − 0.0952 = 90.5% → Healthy
6.2 · Commit Variance
Signed

The rep's actual closed revenue versus what they committed to. Positive means they beat their commit; negative means they missed it.

commitVariance = (closedWon − totalCommit) ÷ totalCommit
≥ 0% Beat / Met −15% to 0% Slight miss < −15% Big miss
📌 Example — Katy
  • Committed AED 300,000 in week 4 · Closed AED 345,000
  • commitVariance = (345k − 300k) ÷ 300k = +15.0% → beat commit by 15%
  • If she had closed AED 240,000: (240k − 300k) ÷ 300k = −20% → big miss
6.3 · Goal Attainment
Vs. admin-set target

Closed revenue divided by the admin-set monthly goal. Goals are configured per rep per month in Manage Goals.

goalAttain = closedWon ÷ monthlyGoal
≥ 100% Goal hit 85 – 99% Close < 85% Behind
📌 Example — Wafiq
  • Monthly goal (admin-set): AED 500,000 · Closed Won: AED 440,000
  • goalAttain = 440k ÷ 500k = 88% → Watch
6.4 · Pipeline Coverage Ratio (PCR)
Forward-looking

How many times the rep's open pipeline covers their monthly goal. The classic SaaS rule of thumb is 3–4× coverage for healthy quota attainment. Always uses the latest month with activity, never summed.

PCR = totalPipeline ÷ monthlyGoal
≥ 4.0× Healthy 2.5 – 3.9× Watch < 2.5× At Risk

Thresholds are admin-configurable in ⚙ Coverage Thresholds.

📌 Example — Jawdat
  • Total open pipeline: AED 1,800,000 · Monthly goal: AED 500,000
  • PCR = 1,800k ÷ 500k = 3.6× → Watch (close to but below the 4.0× green floor)
  • To move into healthy: pipeline ≥ 4.0 × 500k = AED 2,000,000

7Composite Performance Score (0–100)

The single number in the circular badge on each rep card and the primary sort key on the Leaderboard. v13.7 rebalance: Demo Effectiveness joins the formula. Goal Attainment still dominates at 55% of the base score, with Forecast Accuracy, Pipeline Coverage, and the new Demo Effectiveness component each weighing 15%. Reps who exceed 100% goal earn an additional Closer Bonus of up to +10 points — so the rep who actually brings in the AED still beats the rep with the prettiest forecast, but the rep who never runs demos no longer gets a free ride.

Performance Score (v13.7)
goalComp = min(1, goalAttain) — base cap; overachievement handled by bonus
accComp = forecastAccuracy
pcrComp = min(1, PCR ÷ greenThreshold) — 1.0 at 4.0× coverage
demoTarget = max(1, monthsInPeriod × 4) — 4 demos per active month
volComp = min(1, demosRun ÷ demoTarget)
convComp = convRate (= converted ÷ (converted + lost), or 0 if none decided)
demoComp = volComp × 0.40 + convComp × 0.60 — quality > quantity
baseScore = (goalComp × 0.55 + accComp × 0.15 + pcrComp × 0.15 + demoComp × 0.15) × 100
overGoal = max(0, goalAttain − 1) — fraction above 100%
closerBonus = min(10, overGoal × 50) — +1 pt per 2% over, capped at +10
score = min(100, round(baseScore + closerBonus))

11FAQ

Why doesn't my Best Case roll up into Goal Attainment?

Goal Attainment compares actual Closed Won against the admin-set goal. Best Case is a forecast value — it lives in Weighted Forecast and Pipeline Coverage, not in Goal Attainment.

Why does my Forecast Accuracy show 0% in a month I haven't started yet?

If both Weighted Forecast and Closed Won are zero, accuracy is undefined and displays as 0% or "—". As soon as either value moves above zero, the formula activates.

I committed AED 200k and closed AED 350k. Why is my Forecast Accuracy below 100%?

Because the formula is symmetric — over-forecasting and under-forecasting both reduce accuracy. Closing way above commit means the rep was sandbagging. The score rewards calling it correctly, not just over-delivering.

Where do the PCR thresholds (4.0× green, 2.5× amber) come from?

They're SaaS-industry defaults that have proven reliable for B2B sales teams. Admins can change them in ⚙ Coverage Thresholds on the Performance tab if your business model warrants different numbers.

Why does my stale deal still appear on the scorecard?

Stale deals stay visible — they're not deleted. The aging penalty (×0.5) only reduces the Weighted Forecast contribution. The row turns red in the tracker to flag it for review.

How is my XS1 / XP1 forecast scored?

Currently XS1 and XP1 are shown as unit totals on the scorecard but are not factored into the composite Performance Score. They're displayed for trend tracking; an XS1/XP1 actuals-vs-forecast metric is planned.

Where do I edit my monthly goal?

Admin opens the Performance tab → ⚙ Manage Goals → enter the AED target per rep per month. Reps cannot edit their own goal.
